About




I chose art as a hobby and passion at a very young age. I started out taking art class in preschool and elementary school amongst everyone else who had to take it at that age, and when other courses got in the way at school, I devised my own art projects to do at home or I would sketch ideas during my classes. When I got to high school, I made sure to make room for art classes when I could and managed to take physical art classes up until my senior year where I signed up for a class called Multimedia Design. As I learned the basics of Photoshop, Flash, and a bit of coding, I quickly recognized this would be something I would like to pursue further.



In college, I started out as a Graphic Design major at Samford University, and took almost all art classes for about 3 years. While I enjoyed most of the classes, I was not happy at this school as I felt like I didn't belong or didn't fit in. I eventually transfered to Ole Miss and changed majors to Computer Science with hopes of getting an Art minor for all of the art credits I had accumulated from Samford. Although I switched to a more calculating than creative major, I have still managed to find and take some art-related classes such as 3D Modeling and 3D Printing as well as this class, Web Development.
